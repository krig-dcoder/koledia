examination day

march 2026

Today is Monday… and honestly, it’s not giving. It’s already examination week, so the pressure is there, even if I don’t want to admit it.
When I got to school, the first thing I did was talk to my classmates. Just trying to feel normal. Then I heard there wouldn’t be a handwritten exam, and of course, I laughed—like okay nice, less to worry about… or so I thought. XD
But then, we had a hands-on.
So here’s the fun part. I actually reviewed a lot for this, so I thought I was prepared.
At first, I was calm. I thought I was doing okay, just typing my code and going through it like usual. Nothing felt wrong in the beginning.
But suddenly, something felt off. My code wasn’t working, and I had no idea why. That’s when I started getting nervous. I kept checking everything again and again, scanning every line like I was missing something obvious.
Time was running, and I still couldn’t figure it out. The pressure was getting real.
Then finally… I saw it.
A single quote.
Ang liit, but it literally broke everything. I fixed it right away, and thankfully, it worked. I rushed to finish everything because I only had 1 minute left out of 15.
Somehow, I made it.
After that, I just sat there, feeling all the stress leave my body. Today was chaotic, not gonna lie… but I survived. And honestly, that’s enough for now.
I can finally breathe again.

march 2026

Hey…
This one’s a small win. And I don’t think it’s just luck… maybe it’s skill—jk. 😭 But honestly, I feel like this Monday is mine. Two major wins in a row.
It was actually a fun day. I finally got to submit my examination… but of course, it didn’t come easy. There’s always a backstory—especially when it’s me, the cramming girl.
When I arrived at school, I rushed right away. I immediately took out all my materials for soldering—hinang—like I usually do. Everything felt like a work in progress, nothing was really certain yet.
Then time started catching up.
And that’s when I felt it.
I was starting to panic—but in a quiet way. 😭 We were all trying to use the soldering iron at the same time because I misplaced mine.
But I didn’t let that stop me. I stayed focused on my work. I tried to block everything out, even though I was feeling anxious. I wasn’t even sure if what I was doing would work… but I just kept going.
And somehow, at the end of the day, I made it.
My RFID worked.
And when Sir Abenir said my wiring placement was good, that really meant a lot. It felt like all the effort paid off.
Now I’m just hoping my grade this sem turns out well.
But still…
You did good today.
Two wins. One day.
Yeah… this Monday was yours. 😌
