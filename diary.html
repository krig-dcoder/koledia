examination day

march 2026

Today is Monday… and honestly, it’s already examination week, so the pressure is there even if I don’t want to admit it.

When I got to school, the first thing I did was talk to my classmates. Just trying to feel normal for a while.

Then I heard there wouldn’t be a handwritten exam, and for a moment I felt relieved.

But then we had a hands-on.

I actually reviewed a lot for this, so I thought I was prepared. At first, everything felt okay. I was coding normally, going through each step like usual.

Then suddenly, something felt off. My code wasn’t working, and I had no idea why.

I kept checking every line again and again, trying to figure out what went wrong while the time kept running.

Then finally… I saw it.

A single quote. Something so small literally broke everything.

I fixed it immediately and rushed to finish everything. I only had one minute left.

Somehow, I made it.

After that, I just sat there feeling all the stress leave my body. Today was chaotic, but I survived.

And honestly, that’s enough for now.

same day

march 2026

This one feels like a small win.

Monday was surprisingly good to me. Two major wins in one day.

I finally submitted my examination, but of course it didn’t come easy.

Then time started catching up.

I could feel myself getting nervous while everyone was trying to use the soldering iron at the same time, especially after I misplaced mine.

But I just stayed focused on my work. Even though I wasn’t fully confident, I kept going.

And somehow, it worked.

My RFID finally functioned, and when Sir Abenir said my wiring placement was good, it honestly meant a lot.

It felt nice knowing that all the effort paid off somehow.

For now, I’m just hoping this semester turns out okay.

But still… you did good today.

life and works of rizal

may 2026

It’s finals week, and Rizal Life and Works was the only subject where we still had a written exam.

Honestly, I feel like I actually did good this time. I got 48 out of 55.

Maybe it’s still not the highest score for others, but for me, it felt rewarding.

I feel like somehow, I was able to make up for the moments where I felt like I was falling behind.

And honestly, I’m proud of myself for that.

Back in Grade 11, I genuinely didn’t know how to review properly. No one really introduced me to that kind of discipline, so most of the time I just went with whatever I could do.

Sometimes I wonder how different things would’ve been if I learned those habits earlier. Maybe I would’ve become more confident in myself sooner.

But I guess growth still counts, even if it happens late.

And maybe this version of me — the one who’s finally trying — is already becoming someone better than before.

disappearing habit

may 2026

I noticed something about myself lately.

Whenever things start feeling too heavy, too stressful, or emotionally overwhelming, my first instinct is always to disappear for a while.

Not completely, but enough to avoid everything that feels difficult to face.

Sometimes I stop replying. Sometimes I avoid showing up. Sometimes I pretend things don’t bother me even when they actually do.

I think it’s my way of protecting myself when I feel like I can’t handle something properly.

And honestly, I’ve been thinking a lot lately. Wondering if something is wrong with me, or if I’m just too overwhelmed by everything happening all at once.

There are days where my mind feels too loud, even when everything around me is quiet.

But I also know I’m still trying. Even in small ways. Even on the days where I feel lost.

Maybe healing doesn’t always look strong or obvious. Maybe sometimes it just looks like surviving another day and slowly learning yourself along the way.
